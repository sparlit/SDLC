## 5. Strengths Analysis
- **Zero Stub Guarantee:** AST-based enforcement of functional code.
- **Tournament Synthesis:** Error filtering through recursive multi-agent debate.
- **Omniscient Memory:** Vectorized recall of successful architectural patterns.

---

## 6. Weakness Analysis
- **Static Scaling:** Swarm size doesn't adapt to task complexity yet.
- **Cost Observability:** Lack of per-fix token/dollar accounting.
- **Shadow Verification:** Fixes are tested but not in fully isolated gVisor-like sandboxes.

---

## 7. Gap Analysis (Top 3)
1.  **Predictive Scaling Gap:** The difference between static breadth and complexity-aware breadth.
2.  **Hardware Awareness Gap:** Throttling is reactive rather than predictive.
3.  **Cross-Repo Gap:** The swarm is currently limited to the local filesystem scope.

---

## 8. Blind Spot Analysis
- **Implicit Dependency Decay:** Changes in external APIs (like OpenRouter) aren't auto-detected until a failure occurs.
- **Model Bias:** The swarm might favor "popular" patterns over "optimal" ones if not weighted correctly.

---

## 9. Risk Assessment
- **Medium Risk:** LLM cost runaway during intensive debate loops.
- **Low Risk:** "Hallucinated" fixes that pass unit tests but violate business logic.
